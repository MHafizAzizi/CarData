A thousand cars,
one snapshot of
a Malaysian market.

The median car on Mudah.my asks RM 60,800. Toyota holds one in four listings. Two states supply more than half the country's feed. And a dealer, on average, asks 3.81× what a private seller does — though private sellers are nearly invisible on the platform to begin with.

By CarData v2 Source · Mudah.my · EagleSearch API Sample · 5 pages · 1,000 of 86,716 listings
№ 01 — Brand

Toyota holds nearly one in four listings —
the next three brands together don't catch it.

Out of 1,000 cars, 243 are Toyotas. Honda (128), Perodua (122) and Proton (84) — Malaysia's whole national-brand lineage — add up to 334. Above them, only Mercedes shows up at meaningful scale.

№ 02 — Year

2020 is the inflection — between then and the year before,
median prices roughly doubled.

Median asking price for a 2019 car: RM 54,300. For a 2020 car: RM 113,800. Across every other adjacent year-pair, the step is modest; across this one, it more than doubles. Read it as the line between "older used" and "near-new" inventory.

Each point is a median, not a mean — the long right tail of luxury listings (a few cars at RM 1M+) doesn't pull these numbers around.
№ 03 — Region

Selangor & KL together supply seven in ten listings —
the rest of Malaysia shares what's left.

Coverage on Mudah is concentrated. The Klang Valley alone accounts for 69.3% of the sample. Johor follows at 14.7%; Penang, the next largest, holds just 4.5%.

№ 04 — Seller

A dealer asks 3.81× what a private seller does —
yet private sellers post only 9.4% of listings.

The median dealer listing on Mudah is RM 67,750. The median private listing is RM 17,750. The gap is real, though the comparison is uneven: private sellers tend to be older, cheaper cars; dealers stock the near-new inventory.
